Austin Foodie Trip | Pt 1

It all started with a dream. A dream of food, travel, a dream of traveling just for the purpose of food.

Actually, it started with Instagram and donuts. For about 3 years now, I’d been religious looking through #foodporn and #donutporn , and there was one specific donut place in Austin that kept called Sourdough’s Public House that kept popping up. One of my bestie is a fellow foodie, so anytime that either one of us sees a drool worthy picture, we tag each other…for no other reason but to torture ourselves.

Well, one day, one of these routine donut IG tags was fertilized, turned into a little fetus idea, and then BAM! the plan for our foodie Austin trip was born. Before we knew it, we were researching the shit out of Austin, trying to come up with a list of all the places we wanted to try. The mission? Eat at as many places as possible. The catch? We only had 32 hours.

After careful planning, we were ready to go with this list:

  • La Patisserie
  • Snow Monster
  • Gourdoughs Public House
  • El Tacorrido
  • Hey! You gonna eat or what?
  • 24 diner
  • Chilantro
  • Wu Cho
  • Thai Kun
  • Ramen Tatsuya
  • Lucky Robot

However, we started the foodie trip here in Dallas, at MY ALL TIME FAVORITE DONUT SHOP, Jarams. FAM. You DO NOT know donuts until you have had Jarams. “But what you about Krispy Kreme?” BITCHSITDOWN this is #teamnokrispykreme.

Now you may be thinking, “Jennifer, calm down and watch your mouth. They’re just donuts” and to that I say “watch my mouth eat the crap out of these donuts”. Seriously, though...this was the box after like 7 minutes.

Anyways, packed with $40 worth of donuts, my friend and I set off on our 3.5 hour drive, to eat our way through our state’s capitol.

I’ll be writing about each day of our trip in the coming days/weeks, so make sure to stay tuned for the next parts. If you’re planning a trip to Austin or simply like pictures of food porn, you won’t want to miss them.

Okay Baiiii,
